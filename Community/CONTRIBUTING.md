## **3. Contribution Process**

1. **Fork** this repository.  
2. **Create a new branch** for your contribution:  
3. **Add or edit files**, making sure all changes are documented and formatted in Markdown.  
4. **Commit** your changes with a clear message:  
5. **Submit a Pull Request** to the main branch.  
Include:
- A brief description of the contribution  
- Relevant references (whitepaper sections or equations)  
- Any dependencies or tests, if applicable  

All submissions will be reviewed openly by the community or maintainers.  
If the content aligns with the philosophy and is technically accurate, it will be merged.

---

## **4. Quality Standards**

To maintain the credibility of the project, please ensure:

- All claims or explanations are **verifiable** or **referenced** from the official whitepaper.  
- Code contributions include **unit tests** or **simulation results**.  
- Documentation follows **Markdown formatting** (for GitHub readability).  
- English is preferred for technical documentation; translations are welcome in `/Whitepaper/`.

---

## **5. Communication Guidelines**

- Be respectful and constructive in all discussions.  
- Critique ideas, not people.  
- Encourage newcomers and support open collaboration.  
- Keep discussions factual, not promotional.

Toxicity, spam, or misleading statements will lead to removal from contributor channels.
